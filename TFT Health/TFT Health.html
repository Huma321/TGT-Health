Summary

The TFT Health Program Assessment is based on the Health Continuum framework. This framework identifies the main areas where risk is encountered. These areas also provide opportunity to improve health outcomes. The Health Continuum does not consider weighting for factors such as geography or call volume. It is focused on health as it relates to exposure and the associated effects of harmful substances which are inhaled, ingested or absorbed through the skin. Scoring for the

Health Program Assessment ranges from 36 to 108. A score of 36 assesses that a department has significant opportunity to reduce health hazard risk. A score of 108 assesses that a department is taking a high level of action to mitigate risk related to the health of its members. The Health Risk Assessment does not consider all risks that a department and its members are likely to encounter. Additional evaluation should be considered.
